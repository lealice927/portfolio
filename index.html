About Me

Hi! My name is Alice Le and I'm passionate about coding, software, and how technology has encompassed our every day lives. My current interest are in cybersecurity, artifical intelligence, and how the impact and integration of technology has benefitted and helped people become efficient and connected.


I graduated from California State University of Long Beach with a Bachelors in Business Management and Supply Chain Management. My prior occupation in the healthcare industry piqued my interest in software development. Whether that be creating and managing websites that connect medical researchers, developing mobile means of communication that can allow real-time collaboration between physicians in different locations, to creating software that does a better job of compiling and storing patient health records.


Technology and computers have always been an integral part of my life. From building my first computer, being captivated by video games, learning to write my first Javascript function, to building a fully functional run tracking application. I am excited to continue learning and improving my coding skills to become a better developer. I look forward to the opportunity to work alongside experienced developers and contribute to your team.

  • A mobile-responsive application that utilizes modularized React components, were applied to the front-end to organize run-tracking data into independent and reusable UI sections, while a PHP backend was employed to allow calls to create, read, and delete user information and data from the MySQL schema.

    Agile Methodoligies were heavily implemented to separate each stage into effective sprints, and Meistertask was used to stay organized and to prioritize tasks.

    Chart.js was utilized to create dynamic data visualizations of user-movement data, which was obtained through the Geolocation API. The Google Maps API was also implemented to display the current user position during a run.

    The layout of this app was created through Bootstrap to ensure responsive CSS that adjusts to multiple screen sizes.
